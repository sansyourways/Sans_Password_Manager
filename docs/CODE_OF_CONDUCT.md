# SPM Code of Conduct  
Applies to: Issue reporting, bug reports, communication, and private testing  
Version 1.0 — © 2025 Sansyourways

## 1. Purpose
This Code of Conduct exists to ensure professional, respectful, and secure communication between users, testers, and the author of Sans Password Manager (SPM).  
Since SPM is distributed under a **private, closed-source license**, contributions to the codebase are not accepted.  
All allowed interactions must focus only on reporting issues, requesting clarification, or providing feedback.

---

## 2. Expected Behavior
All participants are expected to:

- Communicate respectfully and professionally.
- Provide clear, factual, and reproducible reports when encountering issues.
- Protect their own vaults, keys, and sensitive data.
- Avoid sharing private information in public or semi-public channels.
- Follow the terms of the SPM Private License at all times.

---

## 3. Unacceptable Behavior
The following is strictly prohibited:

- Harassment, insults, discrimination, or aggressive language.
- Attempting to reverse engineer, decompile, modify, or bypass protections in SPM.
- Sharing, redistributing, or publishing the Software or its components.
- Uploading vaults, keys, or sensitive data for others to access.
- Spamming support channels, issues, or emails.
- Making false security claims or spreading misinformation about SPM.
- Attempting to exploit or misuse SPM for unauthorized activities.
- Training AI models using SPM’s code, output, or vault structures.

Any violation may result in permanent ban or termination of your right to use the Software.

---

## 4. Security Reporting
SPM is a security-focused tool.  
If you find a vulnerability:

1. **Do NOT disclose it publicly.**
2. Report privately to:  
   **security@sansyourways.xyz**  
3. Include clear reproduction steps and as much detail as possible.
4. Do NOT include your real vaults, real passwords, or private keys.

All disclosures will be handled responsibly and confidentially.

---

## 5. No Public Contributions
Because SPM is licensed as **private, closed-source software**:

- Pull requests are not accepted.
- Forks are not allowed.
- Modifications or derivative works are prohibited.
- Discussions must remain within permitted channels.

Feedback and bug reports are welcome — source contributions are not.

---

## 6. Enforcement
The author (Sansyourways) has full authority to enforce this Code of Conduct, including:

- Issuing warnings
- Blocking users from communication channels
- Revoking access to beta builds or license
- Terminating use rights under the Private License

Decisions made by the author are final.

---

## 7. Scope
This Code applies to:

- Issue reports
- Feedback channels
- Email communication
- Private testing groups
- Any interaction involving the SPM project or its author
